Services

Defined projects with a written output.

Each project starts with a scoping call to define the document set, workflow, or system, the decision it needs to support, and the finish criteria.

AI Integration and Scientific Automation

For R&D teams whose data acquisition, analysis, or document workflows create manual bottlenecks, and where a generic AI system without materials science grounding creates more risk than it removes.

  • Cited search and extraction over SOPs, SDSs, scientific PDFs, or reports
  • Scientific workflow audit and Python or LabVIEW prototype
  • Human-reviewed AI workflow with failure and review-point report
  • Evaluation sprint with acceptance criteria

Good first project: Scientific workflow audit or technical-document AI discovery pilot.

SBIR and R&D Technical Documentation

For teams that need a materials scientist to pressure-test the technical story before submission, when a weak experimental plan or undefended milestone is the gap between a funded Phase I ($200K-$300K) and a 6-month resubmission cycle.

  • Experimental-plan review
  • Milestone feasibility review
  • DOE-style report and memo support
  • Literature-grounded red-team review

Good first project: Short SBIR technical review or feasibility memo. A $200K Phase I award can become a $1.15M Phase II, so the review pays for itself if it surfaces one weak section before submission.

Advanced Materials Compliance Review

For unusual materials where generic SDS writing misses the science, and where a missed hazard classification or wrong exposure route is the error a distributor or OSHA auditor catches first.

  • SDS/GHS/HazCom technical review
  • Nanomaterial and specialty chemical documentation support
  • Section-level action logs
  • Technical inputs for regulatory teams or counsel review

Good first project: SDS/HazCom triage or one advanced-material SDS technical review.

Materials Characterization and Feasibility

For R&D teams trying to understand why a material or process is not behaving as expected, before committing more time and budget to a direction the characterization data does not support.

  • Characterization interpretation: XRD, SEM/EDX, Raman, FTIR, UV-Vis, PL/TRPL
  • Root-cause or feasibility memo
  • Materials and process review: thin films, perovskites, QDs, scintillators, oxides
  • Technical recommendation path

Good first project: Characterization-backed technical memo.

What a first project typically protects

  • A $750 SDS technical review identifies issues before a distributor rejection, audit, or reformulation cycle, not after.
  • A $1,500 SBIR review catches technical section gaps before submission, when they can still be fixed.
  • A $1,500 AI pilot with an eval report tells you whether the system is trustworthy on your documents before you commit to integration.

These are estimates, not a quote. Work that informs safety or regulatory decisions includes a clear note on what is and is not covered. Typical turnaround: 5-10 business days.

Fit

Clear boundaries make the work better.

The strongest projects have a concrete document set, material system, workflow, decision, deadline, and budget range.

Good fit

  • You have an advanced-material or specialty chemical SDS that needs technical strengthening before a distributor review, audit, or reformulation cycle.
  • You are preparing an SBIR Phase I or II proposal and need a materials expert to review the technical section before submission.
  • You have characterization data (XRD, SEM/EDX, spectroscopy) that needs to become a cited memo, technical review, or decision document.
  • You want to evaluate whether AI can reliably handle your technical documents before committing to a full integration.

Where I don't add value

  • Legal advice or regulatory certification
  • Fully autonomous compliance, HR, billing, or decisions that affect customers directly
  • Generic chatbot with no corpus or acceptance criteria
  • Free sample work, equity-only, or undefined hourly help

Process

A small project should still have a clean handoff.

You get a defined first project and clear options if the review uncovers useful follow-on work.

01

Intake

Define the material, document set, workflow, deadline, budget, and decision.

02

Diagnostic

Set fixed scope, representative files, and acceptance criteria.

03

Written output

Deliver a memo, action log, review, or prototype. Every output includes notes on what it covers and where a human expert still needs to weigh in.

04

Follow-on

Move into rewrite, automation, integration, or deeper review if useful.

05

Handoff

Provide files, assumptions, limitations, and next-step options.
